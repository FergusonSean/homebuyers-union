An owner whose mortgage is paid off at 55 spends their remaining decades in free housing — paying only taxes and maintenance. What your money actually does

Donor contributions go directly into group funds. They shorten the timeline for every member in that group.

Monthly donor contributions are added to the group's fund income alongside member contributions. They are not earmarked for any one member. They raise the water level for the whole group — accelerating the next down payment target, reducing the wait for every person in the queue.

The math is direct: a group's monthly fund growth determines how quickly they can buy the next house. Every dollar of donor contribution is a dollar of additional fund growth per month. It compounds through the lifecycle of the group because every house bought faster means every subsequent house is also bought faster.

For example

$100/month donated to a group of 10 adds $10 per member per month of effective buying power. That's real months cut from real families' timelines — not eventually, but from the very first purchase cycle. Over the full lifecycle of a 10-member group, $100/month in donor contributions could shorten the time to the last mortgage payoff by years.

No returns. No equity. Just impact.

We are going to say this plainly because it matters: this is a charitable gift.

Donors receive no financial return. No equity stake. No claim on any property. No ownership interest in the organization or its assets. When the union dissolves and houses are gifted to members, donors receive nothing material.

This is not a crowdfunded real estate investment. It is not a community investment vehicle. It is not a structured financial product with downside protection. It is a donation to a charitable organization whose mission is to help people own homes.

We say this not as a warning but as a point of pride. The Homebuyers Union exists to serve its members. The only people it is structured to benefit are the families going through the program. Donors make that possible — and that is the entire return.

If you need your money to come back, this is not the right vehicle. If you want to give it — cleanly, directly, with real impact you can follow — you're in the right place.

How funds are governed

Donor contributions are not managed by staff discretion. The structure is designed for accountability.

Managed fund

All contributions — member and donor — flow into a managed fund held separately from organizational operating expenses. The fund exists solely to purchase homes and service mortgages. It cannot be used for salaries, overhead, or administration.

Member-elected board

The board that governs fund allocation is elected by the members. Not by donors, not by founders. The people whose housing is on the line make the decisions. Donor money follows their direction.

Annual transparency reports coming soon

We will publish annual reports showing exactly how much was contributed, how it was allocated across groups, and what outcomes were achieved. Every donor will be able to see — in plain numbers — what their contribution did. We are building the reporting infrastructure now.

Audited financials coming soon

Independent financial audits will be commissioned annually once the organization reaches operating scale. Draft audit policy and auditor selection criteria are in development. We are committed to this as a non-negotiable accountability standard.
